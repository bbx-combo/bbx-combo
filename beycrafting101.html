Beycrafting 101

Beycrafting means building your own Beyblade X combo by choosing parts that work well together. Pick a blade, a ratchet, and a bit. Test them. Change them. Improve them.

What is a Bey made of?

Most Beyblade X combos are built from three main parts.

Blade

The top battle part. It helps decide whether your Bey hits hard, blocks hits, or spins longer.

+

Ratchet

The middle part. It changes height and can affect balance and how easy your Bey is to burst.

+

Bit

The tip part. It changes movement, grip, and spin time.

The original combo is only the beginning

When you buy a Bey, it comes with an original combo — the blade, ratchet, and bit that came together in the box.

That original combo is a good starting point, but it is not always the best combo. The whole idea of beycrafting is to test different parts and discover what works better.

  • Same blade + new ratchet = a different feel
  • Same blade + new bit = a different battle style
  • Testing helps you find stronger combos
Tip: The best combo is often not the original combo from the box. Try different parts and test them in battle.

1. Blades: What do you want your Bey to do?

A blade is the main battle part. Different blade shapes are better at different jobs.

Attack

Dran Sword

Attack blades usually have sharper contact points and more aggressive shapes. They try to hit hard and knock opponents away.

Tip: Choose attack if you want speed, power, and big hits.
Defense

Leon Crest

Defense blades are often rounder and feel more solid. They try to take hits well and stay inside the stadium.

Tip: Choose defense if you want to stay steady and survive attacks.
Stamina

Wizard Rod

Stamina blades usually have smoother, rounder shapes that help them keep spinning for a long time.

Tip: Choose stamina if you want to outspin the other Bey.

2. Ratchets: Number and height

Ratchets have names like 3-60, 5-60, 9-60, or 7-70. The first number tells you the ratchet shape. The second number tells you the height.

Example: 3-60

3 means this ratchet has a three-part outer shape. In this example, you can see three main nubs around the outside.

3-60

What does the first number mean?

The first number is the ratchet shape. Kids often notice this as the number of nubs or sides around the outside.

Common examples are 1, 3, 5, 7, and 9.

Tip: Odd-numbered ratchets are often popular choices.

What does the second number mean?

The second number is the height. Lower numbers sit lower. Higher numbers sit taller.

60 is low, 70 is a bit taller, and 80 is taller again.

Tip: A 60-height ratchet is a great starting point for many combos.

60 Height

Low and stable. This is usually the easiest height to start with.

70 Height

A little taller. Sometimes useful, but also easier to hit.

80 Height

Taller again. It can work in some combos, but it may also feel less stable.

Simple recommendation: if you are not sure where to start, try an odd-numbered 60-height ratchet like 3-60, 5-60, or 9-60.

3. Bits: How does your Bey move?

Bits change movement and spin behaviour. A new bit can make the same blade feel completely different.

Attack Bits

Flatter tips

Attack bits are usually flatter and move faster. They are good for strong hits and knockouts.

Examples: Flat, Low Flat, Rush, Low Rush, Accel, Gear Flat

Defense Bits

More controlled tips

Defense bits are often steadier and help your Bey resist hits or stay in the stadium.

Examples: Needle, Wedge, Hexa, Spike, Gear Needle

Stamina Bits

Rounder tips

Stamina bits are often rounder and smoother so your Bey can keep spinning longer.

Examples: Ball, Free Ball, Orb, Disc Ball

Balance Bits

A bit of everything

Some bits sit between categories and can do a mix of movement, control, and spin time.

Examples: Point, Taper, Unite, Level, Elevate

Tip: If your combo feels wrong, try changing the bit first. It can completely change how your Bey moves.

5. Easy first combo ideas

Attack Idea

Attack blade + 3-60 + Rush-style bit

Good for fast movement and strong hits.

Defense Idea

Defense blade + 5-60 + Wedge/Needle-style bit

Good for staying in the stadium and taking hits.

Stamina Idea

Stamina blade + 9-60 + Ball-style bit

Good for smooth spinning and trying to outlast the opponent.

Final tip: The best combo is the one that works for you. Build it, test it, change it, and test it again.
